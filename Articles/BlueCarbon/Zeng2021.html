157 million % ROI for Indonesian mangroves


While you were busy buying NFT's, Indonesia made a 157,600,000% ROI on their mangroves (do not quote this number). In this article we'll react to this while referring to the paper "Global potential and limits of mangrove blue carbon for climate change mitigation".







Summary




In this paper, Zeng et al. quantify how many mangrove forests could be considered profitable investment vehicles. Ultimately, the research team show that 20% of global mangrove forests are eligible as blue carbon credits (investable projects) and qualify for blue carbon financing.

They highlight that only 10% of global mangrove forests have enough blue carbon credit potential to make their protection financially sustainable over a 30-year time period (they are profitable long-term). This 10% contributes up to 29.8 MtCO2e year−1 and could yield a return on investment of ∼US$3.7 billion per year if it was protected through blue carbon financing.


What does this even mean?


So... in terms of carbon, if we were to finance all the profitable mangrove protection projects, we would only mitigate about 0.13% of annual global carbon dioxide emissions. Now... that might seem insignificant, but it's an important figure.

Why? Because we can give intrinsic value to the area. For example, up to US$513 million could be assigned to Indonesia's at risk mangrove forests, which encourages their protection through blue finance. If the price of carbon went up to ∼US$9.4 t−1CO2e, that value might increase by 10%, and we could generate up to US$3.7 billion of mangrove assets globally... slightly less trivial...




Now, in my opinion, the paper highlights one of the most annoying parts of blue carbon credits: "additionality". In the context of this study, this means that a mangrove area can only be assigned UN blue carbon credits if it is considered to be imminently at risk of destruction.

In other words, globally, 80% of mangroves are not considered to have a tradable blue carbon value yet. That sucks because it doesn't incentivize their protection!




How can we fix this? Well, for starters we need to innovate and develop other conservation financing mechanisms to protect these areas. In the meantime, we should continue to explore mangrove restoration blue carbon projects. Restoration is an important facet of this debate which isn't explored by the paper. Up to 30% of mangroves have been lost globally in the last 50 years and mangrove restoration projects are investable through blue carbon credits!
